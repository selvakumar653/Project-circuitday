Mathematical Shapes

Mathematical shapes are essential in geometry. Here are some common shapes:

  • 2D Shapes: Circles, squares, triangles, and polygons.
  • 3D Shapes: Cubes, spheres, cylinders, and pyramids.

Mathematical Tables

Tables are used to organize and present mathematical data. Here’s an example of a multiplication table for 2:

× 1 2 3 4 5
2 2 4 6 8 10

The Abacus

The abacus is an ancient tool used for arithmetic operations. It consists of beads that represent numbers based on their position in the frame.

To learn how to use an abacus, check out resources on Abacus Master.

Polynomials

A polynomial is an expression involving sums of powers of variables. For example:

P(x) = 2x3 + 3x2 + x + 5

For more on polynomials, visit Khan Academy for in-depth tutorials.
